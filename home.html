ABOUT US

History and Origin

Kisima Jangwani, translating to "Well in the Wilderness," reflects our mission to bring a sustainable water source to a community facing challenges that go beyond the surface. Led by dedicated individuals like Kithinji Muriungi, Esther Muchiri, and @Peter Ebek, our focus goes beyond drilling a borehole. It's about nurturing a thriving community through education, mentorship, and sustainable practices.

Mission and Objectives

Our mission is crystal clear—drill a communal borehole, equip it with solar-powered technology, and enable agricultural activities to flourish. Beyond water, we're committed to mentoring the youth of Kisima through partnerships with Kisima 78 Primary School, Compassion International Kenya, and MCK Kisima 78 Church. We aim to tackle water scarcity, social challenges, poverty, and limited educational opportunities head-on.

Community Impact

The Kisima 78 Village faces water scarcity, social challenges, poverty, and limited education opportunities. Through our project, we're not just solving these problems—we're sowing the seeds of sustainable change. Imagine a community with access to clean water, empowered youth, and thriving agricultural practices. It's not just a dream; it's the reality we're working towards.

Team and Sponsors

Behind every successful project is a dedicated team. Meet the passionate individuals leading Kisima Jangwani: Kithinji Muriungi, Esther Muchiri, and @Peter Ebek. Their diverse backgrounds and expertise drive our mission forward. A heartfelt thank you to our sponsors, IEEE HAC/SIGHT, for believing in our cause and making this journey possible.

Transparency and Accountability

We understand the importance of transparency in charitable endeavors. Rest assured, every donation and resource is utilized with utmost accountability. We provide regular updates on fund allocation and project progress, ensuring that your support directly contributes to the positive change Kisima Jangwani brings.

Future Vision

Kisima Jangwani is not just a project; it's a vision for a sustainable future. Beyond drilling boreholes, we aspire to foster economic activities, provide reliable water sources, and facilitate transformative mentorship programs. Our goal is to create a ripple effect, inspiring sustainable social engagement activities that uplift the entire community.
